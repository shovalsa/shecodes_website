I don't know how to program. Will I have anything to do?
Our goal is to form a community of women with interest in technology and give them the tools to be involved in the industry. We want to increase the number of women who tech, so if you come and persist, you can learn many new things.

What should I bring to the meetings?
You should bring your laptops and headphones.

Where do I register and how much does it cost?
You can register in this link at any given point. You don't have to do this in order to participate. Just come! Participation is free of charge. However, if you want a diploma at the end of the course, it is possible with a nominal fee.

What kinds of meetings are there?
There are three types of meetings:

HackDays/HackNigts - we start out with a short introduction where each participant tells whats she wants to study or do. Then guidance is given for whoever needs one and we just start working.

Insperational Womem Series - women who are leaders in the industry come to share their success stories with us.

Coding Skills Series - women who are leaders in the industry teach us programming and give us important tools to start our own way. Past lectures included introduction to HTML, CSS and JS, iOS development, Android etc.

Tracks: are there any teachers/guides/lectures?
Our tracks contain the best of existing materials on the internet, especially organized and edited into a cohesive program. There is no teacher, but there are well organized video lectures, as well as a mentor and a supporting group.
We start each track once a month. This way, the group of that particular month has roughly the same progress. Moreover, in any given branch, you can find students from former groups who are slightly ahead of you, or even very experienced women, all of which can help you when in need.

Can I come in times other than track openning?
Coming when a track begins is highly important in order to have a smooth learning experience and also to be a part of a community. However, you can benefit from starting in other times, e.g. if you want to accumulate knowledge about the track, or meeting other women.

If I already know some programming, can I skip the introduction or other lessons?
Once you have started the track, you cannot skip lessons. every lesson is very important. If you can't manage to come to your branch in a certain date, you can go to another nearby branch before next class.
If you already know some of the language, you can always either explore deeper in the same issue, or simply help your track-mates.
